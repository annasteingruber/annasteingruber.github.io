Germany

Introduction

As Europe's largest economy and second most populous nation (after Russia), Germany is a key member of the continent's economic, political, and defense organizations.

European power struggles immersed Germany in two devastating world wars in the first half of the 20th century and left the country occupied by the victorious Allied powers of the US, UK, France, and the Soviet Union in 1945.

People And Society

Population

The country has an estimated population of 84,316,622 as of 2022. Population is decreasing 0.11% as of 2022.

Birth rates are 9.08 births per 1000 and death rates are 11.98 deaths per 1000. This explains the decreasing population.

Germany is the 18th most populated country in the world.

Government

Citizenship

There is no citizenship by birth. Citizenship by parentage comes when at least one parent must be a German citizen or a resident alien who has lived in Germany at least 8 years.

Branches of Government

Germany is a federal parliamentary republic. It has a civil law system.

Executive Branch

Chief of State
President Frank-Walter Steinmeier
Head of Government
Chancellor Olaf Scholz

Legislative Branch

The legislative branch is a bicameral parliament which consists of the Federal Council or Bundesrat and the Federal Diet or Bundestag.

Environment

Climate

The climate is temperate and marine, with cool, cloudy, wet winters and summers. There are occasional warm mountain winds.
